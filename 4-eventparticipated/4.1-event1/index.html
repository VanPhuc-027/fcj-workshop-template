Event 1 Offline

Harvest Report: “GenAI-powered App-DB Modernization Workshop”

Event Objectives

  • Introduce cost-effective AWS learning and hands-on tools (AWS Cloud Quest, Floci).
  • Share practical experience and the benefits of participating in Hackathons.
  • Inspire self-confidence for personal and professional development.
  • Present a tech startup case study in astrology (Tu Vi Dai Viet).
  • Raise awareness and provide solutions for hidden pitfalls in software project management and DevOps.
  • Analyze the deeper psychological root causes of procrastination and strategies to overcome them.

Speaker List

  • Huynh Thai Linh
  • Huynh An Khuong, Mai Quoc Anh, Nguyen Tran Nguyen Quan
  • Nguyen Thi Quynh Nhu
  • Nghia Tran
  • Tran Minh Quan
  • Khac Uy Pham

Highlighted Content

1. Level Up Your AWS Skills with Cloud Quest and Floci

  • Speaker: Huynh Thai Linh

  • Beginner Worries with AWS: Beginners constantly worry about unexpected costs, frequently checking billing dashboards out of fear of forgetting to delete resources after hands-on labs, and struggle to understand hidden cloud charges.

  • AWS Cloud Quest: A 3D role-playing game developed by Amazon Web Services (AWS) to make learning cloud computing engaging. It combines theoretical knowledge with hands-on practice, allowing learners to build and validate AWS skills by solving real-world scenarios for virtual characters.

  • Floci AWS Testing: A free, open-source Local Cloud Emulator designed to replace bulky, paid emulation tools due to its rapid startup times and minimal resource consumption. Floci allows users to emulate AWS labs locally without worrying about accruing cloud charges.

  • Floci Limitations: Floci supports a limited set of AWS services and relies on fake results or mock data for certain features, meaning it cannot fully replicate a production AWS environment.

2. HACKATHON

  • Speakers: Huynh An Khuong, Mai Quoc Anh, Nguyen Tran Nguyen Quan

  • Hackathon Overview: Introduced Hackathon as an intense, team-based software development competition held within a continuous, limited timeframe—typically lasting 24 to 48 hours. It functions as a “coding marathon” where members eat, sleep, and build directly at the venue to transform an initial idea into a working Prototype.

  • Why You Should Join a Hackathon: Participating in a Hackathon provides exposure to real-world projects, bridging the gap between theory and practice under intense 48-hour deadline pressures. Winning or participating also adds valuable achievements to build a standout resume/CV.

3. Why We Always Need Confidence

  • Speaker: Nguyen Thi Quynh Nhu

  • What Stays Hidden: A lack of confidence causes us to pass up self-development opportunities, silently bearing unvoiced pressure while hiding core abilities and skills because we lack the courage to showcase them.

  • The Science Behind the Struggle: “Why waste time proving how good you are over and over when you could be getting better?” Identifying the root causes of self-doubt is key to overcoming it. Confidence ultimately makes it easier to build meaningful connections with others.

4. Tu Vi Dai Viet (Vietnamese Astrology Platform)

  • Speaker: Nghia Tran

  • The Problem & Opportunity: Traditional astrology readings cost anywhere from 500k to 2M VND per session but are typically delivered orally without clear structure or verifiable records. The output often contains obscure Sino-Vietnamese terminology that confuses Gen Z users, creating a gap where young people are curious about astrology but deterred by high costs.

  • Vision & Differentiation: “Tu Vi Dai Viet” sets itself apart from standard fortune-telling apps with a continuously updated data engine, user lifecycle tracking, annual forecast compliance, interactive community QA, and being the only platform featuring cross-referencing between Zi Wei Dou Shu and Four Pillars (Bazi).

5. The Hidden Iceberg of a Project: DevOps Before Disaster

  • Speaker: Tran Minh Quan

  • The Hidden Cost of a “Simple Feature”: The speaker shared a memorable project incident where a feature deemed “simple”—originally estimated at 2 days—had to be rewritten 3 times due to miscommunications among stakeholders, ultimately pushing the entire team to exhaustion.

  • The Tip of the Iceberg – Visible Symptoms: Software project management and DevOps resemble an iceberg: visible issues above the surface are minor compared to the massive, dangerous risks beneath. Common symptoms include missed deadlines, production-only bugs, deployment failures requiring emergency rollbacks, dissatisfied end-users, and burned-out engineering teams (Dev/QA).

6. The Iceberg of Procrastination

  • Speaker: Khac Uy Pham

  • A Familiar Enemy: Engaging the audience with 3 relatable questions about procrastination habits:

    • Has anyone here never procrastinated? (annotated with “life-defining moments”).
    • Deadline tomorrow, but scrolling on your phone tonight? “You know who you are, raise your hand!”
    • Watching someone you like walk away… without saying a word? “A missed opportunity we all know too well.”

  • The Iceberg of Procrastination:

    • Visible Tip (20–30%): Laziness—scrolling TikTok/Facebook, snoozing alarms, pushing tasks to tomorrow.
    • Hidden Mass (70–80%): Hidden fears—fear of failure, fear of judgment, or fear of not being good enough.

  • Actionable Advice: Overcome fear by stepping forward boldly, establishing structured work/study routines, and eliminating social media distractions.

Key Takeaways

  • Discovered practical, cost-effective tools for learning AWS, such as AWS Cloud Quest (gamified learning) and Floci (free local emulation), while understanding the limitations of emulators compared to real AWS infrastructure.
  • Realized the strategic value of Hackathons: gaining real-world project exposure under pressure, sharpening teamwork and rapid problem-solving, and building impactful portfolio achievements.
  • Understood the vital role of self-confidence in personal growth—recognizing that self-doubt hides true potential and closes doors to valuable opportunities.
  • Learned to analyze software project management through the “Iceberg Model” in DevOps.
  • Uncovered the psychological drivers behind procrastination, realizing that 70–80% stems from fear rather than mere laziness.

Practical Application

  • Plan to enter future Hackathons to build practical technical experience and strengthen personal portfolio projects.
  • Apply lessons on confidence and overcoming procrastination within our 5-member team: setting granular milestone deadlines, minimizing distractions, and actively communicating ideas.
  • Utilize the DevOps “Iceberg Model” perspective to audit team workflows and mitigate production deployment risks early.

Event Experience

  • Participating in my first offline tech event, I was energized by the vibrant atmosphere and large turnout of students and young tech enthusiasts.
  • The diverse session topics—spanning cloud technicals (AWS, DevOps), soft skills (confidence, overcoming procrastination), and startup case studies (Tu Vi Dai Viet)—kept the event highly engaging.
  • Speakers used an approachable, interactive style filled with humor and relatable stories, making complex concepts easy to digest.
  • The event provided an excellent opportunity to learn from industry veterans while boosting team motivation for our ongoing capstone project.
